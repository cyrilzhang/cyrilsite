Cyril Zhang

(he/him/they/them)

Hi! I'm a researcher at Microsoft Research NYC. My work broadly revolves around theory and algorithms for machine learning. In an immediate research capacity, I care about:

  • Temporality. Learning in dynamical systems and interactive settings. Making algorithms robust to non-stationary environments, particularly via regret minimization. Principles of {meta-, transfer, few-shot} learning.
  • Optimization. Computational-statistical tradeoffs in today's non-convex world. Practical and reusable algorithmic interventions for deep learning. Resource-efficiency beyond conventional asymptotics.
  • Language. My favorite temporal phenomenon. My favorite subproblem in understanding cognition.

In other capacities, I care about fairness, alignment, intersectionality, and boundary objects.

I recently completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science at Princeton, under the supervision of Prof. Elad Hazan. For the latter half of that wonderful adventure, I was a student researcher at Google AI. Before that, I completed a B.S. in Computer Science at Yale, and grew up in Toronto.
